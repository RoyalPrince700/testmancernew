```html
<h2>Basic Sentence Structure</h2>

<p>English sentences follow a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) pattern:</p>

<div style="background-color: #ecf0f1; padding: 15px; border-left: 4px solid #3498db; margin: 20px 0;">
    <strong>Subject</strong> + <strong>Verb</strong> + <strong>Object</strong><br>
    Example: <em>I (subject) eat (verb) apples (object).</em>
</div>

<h3>Common Sentence Types:</h3>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Declarative:</strong> Makes a statement - "I like English."</li>
    <li><strong>Interrogative:</strong> Asks a question - "Do you like English?"</li>
    <li><strong>Imperative:</strong> Gives a command - "Learn English!"</li>
    <li><strong>Exclamatory:</strong> Shows strong emotion - "English is amazing!"</li>
</ul>
```

## Page 2 - Basic English Grammar

```html
<h1>Basic English Grammar</h1>

<div style="background-color: #ecf0f1; padding: 15px; border-left: 4px solid #3498db; margin: 20px 0;">

<h2>What is Grammar?</h2>

<p>Grammar is the system of rules that governs how words are combined to form meaningful sentences. It includes rules for word order, verb tenses, and word forms.</p>

<h2>Parts of Speech</h2>

<p>English words are categorized into different parts of speech:</p>

<div style="display: inline-block; background-color: #f8f9fa; border: 1px solid #dee2e6; padding: 10px 15px; margin: 5px; border-radius: 5px;">
    <h3>üìù Nouns</h3>
    <p>People, places, things, ideas<br><em>Examples: teacher, London, book, happiness</em></p>
</div>

<div style="display: inline-block; background-color: #f8f9fa; border: 1px solid #dee2e6; padding: 10px 15px; margin: 5px; border-radius: 5px;">
    <h3>üèÉ Verbs</h3>
    <p>Actions or states of being<br><em>Examples: run, eat, be, have</em></p>
</div>

<div style="display: inline-block; background-color: #f8f9fa; border: 1px solid #dee2e6; padding: 10px 15px; margin: 5px; border-radius: 5px;">
    <h3>‚ö° Adjectives</h3>
    <p>Describe nouns<br><em>Examples: big, happy, blue, interesting</em></p>
</div>

<div style="display: inline-block; background-color: #f8f9fa; border: 1px solid #dee2e6; padding: 10px 15px; margin: 5px; border-radius: 5px;">
    <h3>üîó Prepositions</h3>
    <p>Show relationships<br><em>Examples: in, on, at, with, for</em></p>
</div>

<h2>Present Simple Tense</h2>

<p>The present simple tense describes habits, facts, and general truths:</p>

<div style="background-color: #d5f4e6; padding: 10px; border-radius: 5px; margin: 15px 0;">
    <strong>Structure:</strong> Subject + Base Verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)<br>
    <strong>Examples:</strong>
    <ul>
        <li>I eat breakfast every day</li>
        <li>She works in an office</li>
        <li>They live in Nigeria</li>
    </ul>
</div>

<h2>Articles: A, An, The</h2>

<p>Articles are small words that come before nouns:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>A</strong> - Used before consonant sounds: a book, a university</li>
    <li><strong>An</strong> - Used before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour</li>
    <li><strong>The</strong> - Refers to specific things: the teacher, the book (we talked about)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Plural Forms</h2>

<p>Most nouns add 's' to form plurals:</p>

<div style="background-color: #ecf0f1; padding: 15px; border-left: 4px solid #3498db; margin: 20px 0;">
    <strong>Regular plurals:</strong> book ‚Üí books, car ‚Üí cars, house ‚Üí houses<br>
    <strong>Irregular plurals:</strong> child ‚Üí children, man ‚Üí men, mouse ‚Üí mice
</div>

<ol>
    <li>My sister ________ (work) in a hospital.</li>
    <li>I eat ________ apple every morning.</li>
    <li>________ children play in the park.</li>
    <li>She has two ________ (child).</li>
</ol>

<div style="background-color: #ecf0f1; padding: 15px; border-left: 4px solid #3498db; margin: 20px 0;">
    <strong>Key Point:</strong> Grammar provides the framework for clear communication. Start with simple structures and gradually build complexity as you gain confidence.
</div>
```
